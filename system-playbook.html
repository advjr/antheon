Companies running paid ads

Your lead response & attribution system

Money is going into ads, but it's hard to say which leads came from which campaign, who followed up, or how fast. Leads sit in a form or an inbox while follow-up happens inconsistently, so budget decisions end up being guesses instead of numbers.

What we'd look at first

  • Every way a lead can reach you: landing pages, forms, calls, DMs, chat widgets
  • How long it currently takes to respond to a new lead, per channel
  • Where campaign and ad data gets lost between your ad account, your site, and your CRM

What usually turns up

  • No consistent first-response time, so leads go cold within the hour
  • Campaign, keyword, and creative data never makes it into the CRM record
  • Follow-up depends on memory, some leads get five touches, others get none
  • No loop back to the ad account, so decisions get made on clicks instead of revenue

Medical & healthcare practices

Your patient intake & care coordination system

New patients call or book online, but intake paperwork, insurance verification, and scheduling live in different tools, or on paper. Front-desk staff re-key the same data by hand, no-shows go unfollowed, and referrals or recall visits fall through the cracks.

What we'd look at first

  • The full patient journey: first contact, booking, intake, insurance verification, visit, follow-up
  • Every place staff re-key the same patient information
  • What happens, if anything, after a missed appointment or an unbooked follow-up

What usually turns up

  • Intake forms collected on paper or PDF, then entered by hand into the practice system
  • No automated insurance or eligibility check before the visit, causing day-of delays
  • No-show follow-up left to staff memory
  • Recall tracking done informally, so patients quietly drop out of care

Construction & contracting companies

Your bid-to-build project tracking system

Estimating, bidding, scheduling, and client updates happen across email threads, spreadsheets, and phone calls. Nobody has one place that shows which bids are out, which jobs are active, or where a project actually stands, and change orders get lost in email.

What we'd look at first

  • The full process from RFP received to estimate, bid, win or loss, scheduling, build, and closeout
  • Where bid data, project status, and client communication currently live
  • How change orders and approvals are documented today

What usually turns up

  • Bids tracked in spreadsheets with no visibility into win rate or deadlines
  • No shared system between office staff and site crews, changes get communicated verbally and lost
  • Change orders approved over email with no paper trail, causing billing disputes
  • Clients calling for status updates because there's no self-serve way to check progress
